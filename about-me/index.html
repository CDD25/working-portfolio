My Journey into Tech.

My Journey into Tech began actually at 10 or 11 years old when my dad showed me how to use the Commodore 64 Vic 20.

As lifelong creative and DIY'er I've always imagined I would have a creative career in design or art of some kind. I decided to go to design school at one point but the cost and talent I believed I needed intimidated me. I honestly didn't believe that I was good enough. My dreams of being a paid creative fizzled away and I turned to more practical (or so I thought) paths like science and healthcare. With a dual Bsc. in Health Sciences and Nursing I became a nurse in 2016. I worked hard and grew in my role, I gained confidence quickly and became a highly compentent clinician. But no one could deny the everyday stress of actually being a nurse. The tireless compassion you often feel for people in tough situations can wear on you. And when they turn on you, which they often did, nursing felt like a thankless profession. After four years I was beaten, and jaded, but I still knew that medicine for me was very exciting, challenging, different everyday, and required an intense amount of critical thinking.

In July 2020 working in the intensive care unit during the height of Covid, I suffered a debilitating back injury that would completely alter my path for the next year. At home for months, I struggled to keep sane and busy while I waited for spine surgery. With my passion for creating, I began taking online classes
